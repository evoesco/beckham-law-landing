Beckham Law Guide

Pay a flat 24% on employment income up to €600,000 and limit Spanish tax to Spain‑source income, for up to six years. Learn who qualifies, what you save, and how to apply.

Eligibility Requirements

  • Not Spanish tax resident in the previous 5 tax years. Tax residence is determined by 183‑day presence, center of economic interests, or family ties.
  • Relocate to Spain for work and acquire Spanish tax residence. Start an eligible pathway soon after arrival: new employment in Spain; transfer to a Spanish entity; remote employee in Spain for a foreign employer; Spanish company director (shareholding limits if no real activity); innovative entrepreneur; highly qualified professional serving accredited startups; or self‑employed in training, research, development, and innovation.
  • Apply within 6 months from start of activity (usually Social Security registration or equivalent).
  • Do not earn income through a Spanish permanent establishment except for the specific self‑employed cases allowed. Extra caution with pass‑through entities.

Tax Benefits

  • 24% flat on employment income up to €600,000 per year. 47% above that.
  • Spain taxes only Spain‑source income. Exceptions: employment and allowed economic‑activity income are taxed in Spain regardless of source.
  • Savings income (dividends, interest, capital gains) taxed at 19–28% as under the ordinary regime.
  • Wealth and Solidarity taxes only on assets located in Spain.
  • No Modelo 720 obligation.
  • Below roughly €55k–€60k of general‑base income the ordinary regime may be better; above that, the special regime often wins.

How to Apply

  1. Phase I — Assess quickly: check 6‑month deadline, confirm advantage, verify requirements.
  2. Phase II — File correctly: compile evidence (causality of move for work), register with Tax Agency via Form 030, submit application electronically with full documentation; legal resolution window 10 business days but typical ≈4–8 weeks.
  3. Phase III — Each year: file the special income tax return April–June for the prior year; assess Wealth/Solidarity returns if applicable.
  4. Phase IV — End of regime: ends on non‑compliance, withdrawal, moving away, or after 6 tax years.

Who Qualifies / Edge Cases

  • Pathways recap: new Spanish employment; intra‑group transfer; remote employee in Spain for a foreign employer; Spanish company director (if no real activity, shareholding < 25%); innovative entrepreneur (ENISA or entrepreneur residence); highly qualified professional serving accredited startups; self‑employed in training/R&D+i.
  • Causality of the move: job offer before arrival or work starting within weeks supports causality.
  • Pass‑through entities risk being treated as Spanish permanent establishment income.

FAQ

  • Duration: up to six tax years.
  • Rate on employment income: 24% up to €600,000, then 47%.
  • What is taxed in Spain: Spain‑source income, with the two exceptions noted above; savings income 19–28%.
  • Modelo 720: not required under this regime.
  • Wealth/Solidarity: only assets located in Spain are considered.
  • Freelancing: only in the specific allowed cases; otherwise incompatible. Consider non‑transparent entities case‑by‑case.
